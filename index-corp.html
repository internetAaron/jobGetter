My Elevator Pitch

Use this space for your elevator pitch. What's an elevator pitch? It's usually a short spoken statement (30-second mini-abstract) about you that lets people know who you are, what you do well, and what you are looking for. It is a well-prepared answer to the questions: "Tell me a little bit about yourself," or "So, what do you do?"

Things I Love

Use this section to talk about who you are as a person. Talk about what really motivates you, what you do in your spare time, hobbies and anything else that doesn't relate exactly to your work career.

If it takes two or three paragraphs that's fine. Just talk about your family, passions and anything else that keeps you going.

What I Do

Time to get down to business. Talk about what you do for a living. Outline your current jobs, tasks and responsibilities. Be positive but be honest. Don't talk bad about your coworkers or the company that you currently work for. No matter how bad things are you don't want to come across that way.

Also be sure to talk about any extra work/experience you have. If you do freelance work or contribute to open source projects be sure to mention that too.

    Don't just make some stuff up here. Do your research on the company. Learn everything you can about them so you can touch on all the right topics.

    Find Common Values

    Read blog posts, About Us pages and anything else you can to find shared values. Then take that and explain how you share that same value and how excited that makes you.

    Talk about how great the company is. Everyone like to hear good things about their company. It will also show them where you see value in the company.

    Hit on Topics from the Job Posting

    Read through the job posting and find you strong points and talk about them here. Remind them why you are the right person for the job.

    Talk about how your skills meet and/or exceed the job description. You can evenbold the skills that you have that match the description
